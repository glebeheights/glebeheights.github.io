Article I

Purpose of the Association

Section 1: The Glebe Heights Community Association (GHCA), Inc. hereinafter referred to as the Association, is a non-profit association of property owners and residents of Glebe Heights, Edgewater, County of Anne Arundel, and State of Maryland, duly incorporated under the laws of the State of Maryland as a non-stock and non-profit corporation, for the purpose of furthering the interests of said property owners, and of maintaining and administering the affairs of the Association.

Article II

Seal

Section 1: The Association shall have a seal, which shall be in the form of a circle with the following words thereon: “GLEBE HEIGHTS COMMUNITY ASSOCIATION, INC., 1972, MARYLAND”.

Article III

Membership

Section 1: Every person who owns property or resides in Glebe Heights, Edgewater, Anne Arundel County, Maryland, as of January 1st, 2017 may be eligible for membership in the Association solely by virtue of his or her status.

Section 2: Membership may be obtained by payment of an assessment and shall be limited to 75 members. If membership interest exceeds the current limit, a waiting list will be established. Memberships will automatically renew if the assessment is paid by the June 1st deadline.

Section 3: One vote allowed per paid member present at a meeting, limited to a maximum of two (2) votes per household.

Article IV

Conduct of Meetings

Section 1: Questions respecting the conduct of meetings, propriety of discussion, elections, etc. which are not specifically covered by these by-laws, shall be governed by “Roberts Rules of Order”.

Article V

Elected Officers

Section 1: Elected officers of the Association shall be a President, a Vice President, a Treasurer, a Secretary, Chairman of the Beach Committee, Chairman of the Roads Committee, and Chairman of the Ways and Means Committee, all of who shall be elected by secret ballot.

Section 2: All elected officers of the Association shall hold office for two years or until their Successors qualify.

Section 3: Any officer who fails to attend three consecutive meetings without a valid excuse shall be terminated and a successor appointed by the Board of Directors.

Article VI

Elections

Section 1: In April of the election year the President shall appoint a Nominating Committee consisting of five (5) members, excluding current officers. At the May meeting, the Nominating Committee shall present a slate of officers who have agreed to serve if elected. Nominations may also be taken from the floor until elections are held at the June meeting. This committee shall prepare written ballots and provide space for the write-in of candidates by the membership.

Section 2: To be eligible as a candidate for any elected office, the person must be an assessment-paying member in the Association for two (2) consecutive years, and a citizen of the United States.

Section 3: No person shall be elected by absentee vote or vote by proxy. The outgoing officers shall continue the Election Meeting until all officers are elected. At that time the outgoing President shall introduce all new officers. The new President shall adjourn the meeting.

Article VII

Board of Directors

Section 1: The Board of Directors shall consist of the following: President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Chairman of the Beach Committee, Chairman of the Roads Committee, and Chairman of the Ways and Means Committee.

Section 2: Meetings of the Board of Directors shall be held at such time and place as the Board may determine.

Section 3: Any Board of Directors’ meeting shall require five (5) members to constitute a quorum. In the absence of a quorum the presiding officer shall adjourn the meeting to such time and place as may be determined by the members present, notice of which shall be given to the absent members.

Section 4: The Board of Directors reserves the right, after due opportunity to be heard and upon at least thirty (30) days notice, at either a regular or a special meeting to suspend or expel a member by two-thirds majority vote of those present and voting for any act or conduct deemed detrimental to the best interests of the Association. The notice shall be sent by mail to the member at his address as it appears upon the records of the Association.

Section 5: The Board of Directors shall have the power to remove or cause to be removed buildings, derelicts, dilapidated or unsafe or unsightly piers or boats or other craft that are attached to or on property under the Association’s jurisdiction, according to law. The Board of Directors may also remove or cause to be removed any property, such as furniture, fishing and crabbing equipment, tools or other items not property of the association without notice after a period of Seventy-two (72) hours.

Section 6: The Board of Directors shall have the power to enforce all provisions within these By-Laws and amendments. Violations of any terms shall result in a mailed letter from the Board to the member outlining the violation and the means in which it should be corrected. Three (3) violations in one calendar year will result in a mandatory vote by the board to determine the members continued status with the Association. Penalties for continued violations of the Association By-Laws are at the sole discretion of the Board, to include loss of boat/kayak clip or slip seniority, dismissal, and future membership.

Article VIII

Meetings

Section 1: Regular Meetings. Meetings will be held on the second Monday of each month at 6:30pm at the community beach or other designated area. The membership will be given adequate notice of any change.

Section 2: Special Meetings. A special meeting of the members of the Association may be called by the President, the Board of Directors, or by a petition signed by ten (10) members of the Association. The said special meeting shall be held at a designated area in Glebe Heights, Edgewater, Anne Arundel County, and State of Maryland. The call for the meeting shall state the purpose and object of the meeting. No other business than that stated in such notice shall be transacted.

Section 3: Quorum. The presence of seven (7) members at a meeting of the Association shall constitute a quorum for the conduct of business, and in the absence thereof the President shall adjourn the meeting.

Section 4: No member shall be allowed to speak more than once nor longer than (5) minutes on the same motion to the exclusion of others unless to explain, and then he must confine himself to explanation and not debate the main question.

Article IX

Assessments

Section 1: All assessments for membership shall be set by the Board of Directors.

Section 2: Assessments will be tiered to reflect use of the major association amenities. Basic membership will include access to all areas of the property. Boat Ramp access and use will require an additional assessment. Boat Slip access and use will require the member to pay the Boat Ramp assessment and an additional assessment based on the size of the vessel being maintained at the slip. A minimum slip fee is required based upon a twelve (12) foot vessel.

Article X

Use of Association Property

Section 1: Persons not members of the Association will be denied use of the beach, grills, fire pit, piers and boating facilities, including boat ramp and any future improvements erected and maintained by authority of the Association.

Section 2: Each assessment-paying member shall be given one Identification Card. Guests must be accompanied to the beach, pier, boat ramp and facilities by a member who shall be responsible for his guests’ actions and conduct. Also must, while being responsible, be held liable for costs payable for any damage to property or any item maintained by this Association.

Section 3: Each assessment paying member shall be given one (1) key to the gate lock and one (1) key to the boat ramp lock depending on level of membership. Keys SHALL NOT be duplicated and SHALL NOT be given to any person other than the current members. Keys MUST NOT be given to guests under any circumstance.

Section 4: Members holding boat slips must have eight (8) hours of community service per year (June 1 to May 31). Members holding Kayak slips must have four (4) hours of community service per year (June 1 to May 31). Examples of such community service include, but are not limited to: cutting grass, maintenance on pier and/or sea nettle nets, picking up supplies, assisting in fund raisers and so forth. This time must be turned in to the President on a weekly basis or as performed. Board Members will determine violations with fines up to $25.00 per hour for each hour of community service or dismissal of boat slip privileges. Illness and hardship will be a factor considered in this decision.

Section 5: Property owners may not rent boat slips with the rental property in the community. The Renter(s) must be placed on the Association Waiting List as do all property owners.

Section 6: Association members holding a boat slip are permitted to lodge only those boats that are owned by the member. A member can reserve a slip if the member owns a boat, or a boat purchase is anticipated, however, they may not rent, lease, or loan the slip to other individual(s) within or outside of the Association without permission from the Board. Any member with a boat slip must have a boat within two years, effective June 9, 2025, or they must forfeit the boat slip. Owner must show proof of registration. If member does not have a boat during this time frame, member will move down to the wait list, for the following year, based on seniority. Failure to comply may result in dismissal of boat slip privileges as determined by the Board members.

Section 7: Boat and Kayak slips are available to members for an additional assessment. If available slips do not exist, a waiting list will be formed for the next available slip. Members will be assigned slips on the basis of seniority on the slip list. Boat slip fees are determined by the board each year and assessed per foot, with a minimum size of twelve (12) feet. Kayak slip fees are determined by the Board each year and assessed per rack slot. Slip assignments will be made after the June 1st, yearly slip dues deadline. Previous slip holder will lose their place in the seniority list if dues is not received by the June 1st deadline.

Article XI

Duties of Officers

Section 1: The President shall preside at all meetings of the Association and of the Board of Directors. The President shall have the power to limit debate on all questions or motions; maintain order at all times; insist that all debates be on the question at hand; not grant the floor to the same person twice to talk on the same question until each member present has had an opportunity to voice his or her views or opinions. The President, with the approval of the Board of Directors, shall appoint all committee chairmen, except as otherwise provided in these by-laws. The Chairman of each committee shall make a report to the membership at the regular meetings on the committee’s activities. The President shall be equally responsible with the Treasurer and Secretary for the care and custody of all documents of the Association, said documents to be kept in such place as shall be designated by the Board of Directors. He shall also appoint an Audit Committee of three (3) for a yearly audit.

Section 2: The Vice President shall, in the absence of the President, or in the event of inability to act, perform the duties of the President. In the event of absence or inability of both the President and Vice-President to act, the Association shall elect a President, pro tem, who shall preside.

Section 3: The Treasurer shall have the care and custody of the funds of the Association; be equally responsible with the President for the care and custody of all documents of the Association; have the power to sign checks and vouchers for the payment of monies; send out bills; keep correct books of the financial affairs of the Association; report the condition of said finances to the membership at every meeting; keep the books open at all times for inspection by the Board of Directors. The Treasurer, with the concurrence of the President is authorized to expend up to $300 without the full vote of the membership, to conduct regular business, including utilities, maintenance, and event supplies.

Section 4: The Secretary shall keep minutes of all meetings; keep the Seal of the Association and the roster of members; send out all notices and carry on all correspondence, etc.

Article XII

Special Officers

Section 1: All recommendations pertaining to the appointment of Special Officers of Glebe Heights in the name of the Association shall be approved by the membership at a regular meeting, and certification of approval shall be by two elected officers of the Association to the appointing authorities.

Article XIII

Amendments to the By-Laws

Section 1: Any amendment to the By-Laws must be submitted in writing to the membership and read at three (3) consecutive meetings. The amendment is to be voted upon at the third meeting. A two-thirds majority is required for acceptance.

Section 2: Any change to a proposed amendment offered at the time an amendment is presented at a general or special meeting, if adopted, will constitute and be construed as a new amendment, which then must be presented in full conformity with Section 1 hereof.

Article XIV

Authority of These By-Laws

Section 1: These By-Laws shall become effective when approved by more than two-thirds of those members present and voting at a meeting called by the membership for the sole purpose of approving these By-Laws and shall replace all previous By-Laws of the Association.

Article XV

Addendum (Beach Rules)

Section 1: Added to these By-Laws are all rules incorporated in regards to the Community Beach operation. These rules shall be available for all members. Notice shall also be posted on the beach.

  • Admittance to the beach by members and accompanied guest only.
  • Admittance to pier by members and accompanied guest only.
  • Boat ramp for use only by paid up members of the Association ramp maintenance fee.
  • Parking of cars for members only in designated areas. Members will be assigned two (2) Parking Passes per household and must display those passes in the front windshield of the vehicle when parked on the association property. Failure to display may result in the towing of the vehicle at the owners expense.
  • Open fires ONLY allowed in the designated fire pit AND MUST be guarded by a member. All fires should be completely extinguished before leaving the property.
  • No one allowed on beach after 11 pm (unless due to circumstances beyond control.)
  • All picnic tables, etc. must always be neat and clean with no trash of any kind to be tossed around.
  • No animals allowed on beach. Animals are allowed on other areas of the property but must be cleaned up after promptly.
  • This is a private beach owned and maintained by the GHCA, Inc. Any defacing of property, damage of any means, can and will be enforced by law.
  • There will be no boat trailers left at the beach property for more than twenty-four (24) hours. Violators will be subject to towing at the owners expense.
  • No alcohol will be allowed on Glebe Heights Association Property. Special exceptions must be preapproved by the Board and in accordance with Maryland State Law.

By-Laws approved by 2/3rds vote on 11/14/2016. Revised June 9, 2025.
